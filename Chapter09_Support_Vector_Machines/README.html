9.1.1 What is a HyperPlane?

In a p-dimensional space, a hyperplane is a flat affine subspace of dimension p-1 which is defined by the equation

\(\beta \)0 + \(\beta \)1X1 + \(\beta \)2X2 + + \(\beta \)pXp = 0 (9.2)

We can see that the hyperplace divides p-dimensional space into two halves and by simply calculating the sign of the left hand side of (9.2), one can determine on which side of a hyperplane a point X = (X1,X2,,Xp)T lies.
